💡 Writing Feedback

🌅 Beginning - Exposition (Setting the Scene)

📚 Literary Term: Exposition - The part of the story where you introduce characters, setting, and background information.

Introduce your guardian and the ocean world. What does it look like? What's happening?

💡 Tip: Use descriptive words to paint a picture of the underwater world

⚡ Problem - Rising Action (The Challenge Appears)

📚 Literary Term: Rising Action - The part where the main conflict or problem is introduced and tension builds.

What problem does your guardian discover? What threatens the ocean?

💡 Tip: Make the problem feel urgent and important

🏃‍♀️ Action - Climax (The Guardian Takes Action)

📚 Literary Term: Climax - The most exciting part of the story where the main character faces the biggest challenge.

How does your guardian try to solve the problem? What adventures happen?

💡 Tip: Show your guardian using their special powers and personality

📉 After the Action - Falling Action (What Happens Next)

📚 Literary Term: Falling Action - Events that happen right after the climax, showing consequences and character reactions.

What happens immediately after your guardian's big action? How do others react?

💡 Tip: Show the immediate effects and how characters react to what just happened

🌟 Ending - Resolution (Problem Solved)

📚 Literary Term: Resolution - The ending where conflicts are resolved and loose ends are tied up.

How is the problem solved? What happens to your guardian and the ocean?

💡 Tip: Show how your guardian has grown or what they learned
